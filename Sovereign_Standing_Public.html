The standing question: three currency classes are competing to anchor the next monetary era — decentralized Network Coins(by default unstable coins), stable coins, and central bank digital currencies. They all have a place. Which one anchors the paradigm is the question this board tracks, with sources on every square.

The board — state of play

① Network Coins(by default unstable coins)

  • U.S. Strategic Bitcoin Reserve holds ~330K BTC under a no-sell executive order; sixteen nations have proposed or enacted strategic reserves.
  • First-ever joint SEC/CFTC release classified a group of digital assets as commodities; nine coins trade CFTC-regulated CME futures.
  • The open square: reserve-asset treatment is won — no sovereign yet settles over a network-coin rail.

② Stable Coins

  • GENIUS Act (2025) made the class official U.S. framework; USD tokens ≈99.8% of the entire stablecoin universe.
  • Final implementing rules due ~July 18, 2026; regime effective no later than January 2027.
  • The square to read: freeze, seize, and programmability provisions in the final text.

③ CBDC

  • mBridge (CN·HK·TH·UAE·KSA): $55B+ settled, ~95% of volume in digital yuan, post-BIS.
  • Digital ruble acceptance mandates phasing in; digital euro pilot path 2027→2029; BRICS Pay targets 2030.
  • The U.S. posture: the Anti-CBDC Surveillance State Act passed the House — the reserve issuer building no sovereign rail of its own.

The board's quiet asymmetry: the class with no issuer is the only one whose rules cannot change between your deposit and your withdrawal. Every other square on this board is a promise with an amendment process.
