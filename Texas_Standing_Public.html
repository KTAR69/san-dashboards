The three instruments

Texas Stock Exchange (TXSE)

  • Began trading July 6, 2026 — the first well-funded new U.S. national exchange launch in decades; $275M backing incl. BlackRock and Citadel Securities.
  • Phased rollout: all NMS symbols through July · ETPs Q3 · corporate listings Q4 · IPOs 2027.
TXSE · Texas Tribune

Texas Strategic Bitcoin Reserve (SB 21)

  • Signed June 2025; a special fund held OUTSIDE the state treasury, administered by the Comptroller of Public Accounts (fund #1018).
  • Statutory filter: eligible assets require ≥$500B average market cap over 24 months — a network-coin standard written into state law.
Comptroller Manual of Accounts · SB 21 text

Bullion Depository + HB 1056

  • The first state-run precious-metals depository in the U.S. custodies the reserve behind it.
  • HB 1056: gold and silver specie restored as legal tender, spendable through a depository-backed digital currency — effective September 1, 2026.
HB 1056 (Texas Legislature)

The observation this board exists to test: one state currently holds network-coin reserves, physical gold, and a live digital hard-asset rail — while prohibiting none of the competing classes. That's a diversified monetary posture no national government has attempted. Whether it's foresight or coincidence is what the milestones will tell us.
The instrument to watch isn't the exchange — it's the depository rail. Exchanges move paper claims; the rail moves settlement. September 1 is the only date on this page that changes architecture instead of venue.
